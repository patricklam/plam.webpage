Singapore

July 18, 2006

My main tasks in 2006 were to finish my PhD dissertation and to defend it. To gain teaching experience, I also taught a summer course at McGill, Programming Languages and Paradigms.

It is useful to occasionally show drafts of one's dissertation to one's advisor and collect feedback. The best way to get feedback from Martin is to present him with the document in question; fortunately, he provides the feedback while-you-wait. Other ways of getting feedback have no termination guarantees.

Of course, this means of getting feedback doesn't work very well when I'm not in the same place as Martin. As noted above, one complication was that I was in Montreal for two months; Martin was also travelling quite a bit, for instance to the West Coast of the US, to Singapore (for the Singapore-MIT alliance), and to the Maldives.

So, I decided to tack a one-week trip to Singapore onto my trip to Germany for Alex's wedding. This worked out quite well and I got a chapter or two past Martin on the trip. But the best line was this: while waiting for my flight in the Frankfurt airport, I was talking to some students who were travelling from the middle of the United States to India, via some rather complicated 3-day routing that would save them a couple hundred dollars.

Student: "Are you travelling for business or pleasure?"

Me: "I'm going to Singapore to work on my thesis with my advisor."

Third party: "That cannot possibly count for pleasure!"
