617-4

June 1, 2008

Following dom and Ruth's wedding, MP and I stayed at the Westin Waltham-Boston, a fancy hotel. I quite enjoyed it. However, as befits fancy hotels everywhere, they charge (a lot) for everything; for instance, we ended up having a $40 breakfast! Of course there is no free internet or local phone access. Only cheap (i.e. low-to-midrange) hotels have such things for free. I'd say that fancy hotels like to nickel-and-dime their guests, if by nickel and dime I mean $5-and-$10.

In any case, the plan was to go to the wedding on Saturday (ribs! pie! disco! what more could we want? go dom and Ruth!), and then to go hiking at Killington with Dave on Sunday. I thought it would be a good idea to call Dave and reconfirm our plans. OK, fine, it costs $1.50.

I start dialing his number on the room phone (since there are no Canadian cellphone plans with reasonable rates for use in the US), but misdial 617-4 and realize that there are no 4s in Dave's phone number, so I hang up and then dial the right number. No answer. OK, never mind then. He'll just show up at 8 and we'll go hiking.

Next morning, as you can see in the bill, I find out that billing system for the phone is quite zealous and charges me $6.93 for dialing 617-4. That's not even a phone number! How is it reasonable to charge $6.93 for dialing 4 digits?!

Fortunately, the front desk person quickly reversed that charge when I checked out, but c'mon! She even said something about the phone rates being next to the phone. No, posted phone rates do not include non-calls.

Postscript

I later check my credit card and find that they charged $6.94 to it after I checked out. Bastards! They claim that it was for the minibar, which is bogus, but they also (claim to have) reversed it.
